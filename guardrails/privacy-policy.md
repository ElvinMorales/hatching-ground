# Privacy policy

Hatching Ground is private-first, but a repository is not a private vault. Store real working material only in ignored local/private folders with appropriate device controls.

## Must stay private

Personal and family details, health and financial information, employer information, private communications, source documents, account identifiers, browsing history, and any combination that can identify or profile a person must remain private.

## Never commit

Never commit credentials, API keys, tokens, passwords, `.env` secrets, real private data, raw logs, memory stores, state snapshots, private system screenshots, machine paths, or exports from personal or employer systems. If any are committed accidentally, stop, remove them from history as appropriate, rotate exposed credentials, and review access.

## Synthetic examples

Shared examples must be invented, minimal, and non-identifying. Changing only a name is not enough: replace dates, quantities, places, organizations, filenames, and distinctive combinations. Label examples synthetic. Do not derive realistic samples from one person's record.

## Sensitive domains and human review

Hatching Ground may organize, summarize, draft, reflect, plan, and propose questions in health, legal, financial, relationship, or employment contexts. It must not make decisions or take actions in those domains. A human must review every consequential recommendation and approve any later write, send, commit, delete, purchase, contact, or external change.
